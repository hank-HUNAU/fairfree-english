1. Large Deep Orebodies
Large ore-bodies are often known as massive, but the term massive should not be confused with massive ore块状矿石 and disseminated ores浸染状矿石 in a geological sense. In fact a massive ore-body is likely to contain disseminated ores浸染状矿石 minerals. A vein/veɪn/ 矿脉-type ore-body will probably contain bands of massive ore块状矿石 minerals.
A shaft/ʃɑːft/ 竖井 is sink/sɪŋk/ 掘进 alongside or through the ore-body for access. In practice, there must be two openings to allow air to be forced through the mine workings. It must be noted that if the shaft/ʃɑːft/ 竖井 is sink/sɪŋk/ 掘进 through the ore-body then some of the ore is sterilize/ˈsterɪlaɪz/ 保安矿柱d. A cone of ore surrounding the shaft/ʃɑːft/ 竖井 and widening with depth must be left until the rest of the ore is extraction/ɪkˈstrækʃən/ 开采ed. Otherwise the movement of rock caused by ore extraction/ɪkˈstrækʃən/ 开采 will damage the shaft/ʃɑːft/ 竖井 and necessitate expensive repairs, and lost production.
Waste rock, ore, men and materials have to be raised and lowered in the shaft/ʃɑːft/ 竖井s. There are two types of conveyance, cage/keɪdʒ/ 罐笼s and skip/skɪp/ 箕斗s. Cage/keɪdʒ/ 罐笼s are used for man-riding and to wind mine cars containing ore, waste rock or materials.

2. Vein-type Ore-body
A large ore-body may in fact consist of cluster/ˈklʌstə/ 群s of smaller ore-bodies of a vein/veɪn/ 矿脉-type, and development may be on a regular grid. Alternatively/ɔːlˈtɜːnətɪvli/ 或者 the ore-body may be one predominant/prɪˈdɒmɪnənt/ 主要的 vein/veɪn/ 矿脉 which is long and narrow on its strike/straɪk/ 走向 (horizontal) direction and which extends to the dip for a considerable depth. Technically the strike/straɪk/ 走向 is at right angles to the full dip, but within an orebody the strike/straɪk/ 走向 may follow a sinuous/ˈsɪnjuəs/ 弯曲的 line and the dip frequently varies so that general trends have to be taken.

It can also be appreciate/əˈpriːʃieɪt/ 理解d that mining operations would weaken the hanging wall上盘 i. e. the rock lying above the ore body, and cause it to collapse. As a result the major developments are normally made in the footwall/ˈfʊtwɔːl/ 下盘, below the orebody, where the rock is more stable.
