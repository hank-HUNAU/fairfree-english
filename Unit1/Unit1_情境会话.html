Scene 01
野外地质勘查 — Field Geological Survey
两位工程师在矿区野外考察，观察岩层结构，讨论褶皱、向斜、背斜及整合层等地质构造。
Z Dr. Zhang — 地质工程师
L Mr. Li — 采矿工程师
Z
Dr. Zhang
Mr. Li, take a look at this rock strata岩层 section. The simplest form is where each stratum地层 is superimposed叠加 on another. These are conformable beds整合层.
L
Mr. Li
I see. So the layers have been deposited in a regular sequence without any significant break. But what about those curved formations over there?
Z
Dr. Zhang
Good observation. Those are folds褶皱. The beds have been distorted by movements of the earth's crust地壳运动. This downward curve here is a syncline向斜, and the upward arch is an anticline背斜.
L
Mr. Li
The scale is impressive. How large can a single fold be?
Z
Dr. Zhang
A single fold producing a synclinal basin向斜盆地 may stretch for hundreds of kilometers. Or it may be measured with a wavelength of just a few meters. The scale varies enormously.
L
Mr. Li
I notice the rock here is heavily fractured断裂的, especially near the crest of that anticline.
Z
Dr. Zhang
Exactly. Where the folding is severe, the rock is frequently heavily fractured, particularly where it is under tension张力 on the crest of an anticlinal stratum or at the base of a synclinal stratum.
L
Mr. Li
So these weakened zones弱化带 could provide a path for mineral-rich solutions to permeate the host rock?
Z
Dr. Zhang
Precisely. These weakened zones provide a path for igneous intrusive rocks火成侵入岩 or mineral-rich solutions to permeate the host rock围岩 and produce ore bodies矿体. That is why many ore bodies have a typical form, such as saddle reefs鞍状矿脉.
L
Mr. Li
That makes sense. What about the faults断层 in this area? I can see several fracture lines running through the formation.
Z
Dr. Zhang
In practice, a fault of this magnitude would have many associated smaller faults and fractures that can again give rise to an area favourable for subsequent mineralisation矿化. These are the areas we should focus on for exploration.

张博士：李先生，看看这个岩层剖面。最简单的形式是每一层岩石叠加在另一层之上。这些就是整合层。
李先生：我明白了。也就是说这些岩层是按规则顺序沉积的，没有明显的间断。但那边那些弯曲的构造是什么？
张博士：观察得很好。那些是褶皱。岩层被地壳运动扭曲了。这个向下弯曲的是向斜，向上拱起的是背斜。
李先生：规模真令人印象深刻。一个褶皱能有多大？
张博士：产生向斜盆地的单个褶皱可能延伸数百公里。或者其波长可能只有几米。规模差异很大。
李先生：我注意到这里的岩石严重断裂，特别是在那个背斜的顶部附近。
张博士：没错。当褶皱剧烈时，岩石经常严重断裂，特别是在背斜岩层顶部受张力处或向斜岩层底部。
李先生：所以这些弱化带可能为富含矿物的溶液渗透围岩提供了通道？
张博士：完全正确。这些弱化带为火成侵入岩或富含矿物的溶液渗透围岩并形成矿体提供了通道。这就是为什么许多矿体具有典型形态，如鞍状矿脉。
李先生：有道理。那这个区域的断层呢？我看到好几条断裂线穿过这个构造。
张博士：实际上，这种规模的断层通常伴有许多较小的断层和裂隙，它们同样可能形成有利于后续矿化的区域。这些正是我们勘探应关注的区域。

Scene 02
矿体分析讨论 — Orebody Analysis
两位工程师在实验室讨论矿石类型、脉石、品位界限及矿石分类等专业知识。
Z Dr. Zhang — 地质工程师
L Mr. Li — 采矿工程师
Z
Dr. Zhang
Let's discuss the classification of this orebody矿体. An orebody may contain both desirable metallic minerals and unwanted non-metallic minerals. The metallic minerals form the ore矿石, and the unwanted minerals are known as gangue脉石.
L
Mr. Li
I understand. So we need to separate the ore from the gangue during processing. But how do we define the boundary of an orebody?
Z
Dr. Zhang
That is where the concept of cut-off grade边界品位 comes in. A gradual transition of high grade to low grade ore is common, and it is not always easy to determine the physical limits of an ore body.
L
Mr. Li
So the cut-off grade is the grade below which the material is no longer economically worth mining?
Z
Dr. Zhang
Exactly. An orebody should be known more accurately as mineralisation矿化 which comprises economic ore and sub-economic次经济的 lower grade material. The lower grade material broken during mine development is called mullock废石 or waste.
L
Mr. Li
What about the physical form of the ore? I have heard the terms massive ore块状矿 and disseminated ore浸染矿.
Z
Dr. Zhang
Where the ore occurs in relatively pure form — for example, as almost 100 percent lead sulphide硫化铅 (galena方铅矿) — even in small amounts, it is said to be massive.
L
Mr. Li
And disseminated ore is the opposite — the ore is spread thinly through the body of rock?
Z
Dr. Zhang
Correct. If the ore is spread thinly through a body of rock so that individual specks颗粒 of the ore are surrounded by a high percentage of rock, then this is a disseminated ore. Massive ore is generally easier to process, but disseminated ore can still be economic if the deposit is large enough.
L
Mr. Li
That is very helpful. Now I have a clearer picture of how we classify and evaluate an orebody.

张博士：我们来讨论一下这个矿体的分类。矿体可能含有有价值的金属矿物和不需要的非金属矿物。金属矿物构成矿石，不需要的矿物被称为脉石。
李先生：我明白了。所以我们在加工过程中需要将矿石与脉石分离。但我们如何确定矿体的边界呢？
张博士：这就是边界品位的概念。高品位到低品位矿石的逐渐过渡很常见，确定矿体的物理界限并不总是容易的。
李先生：所以边界品位就是低于该品位后，材料在经济上不再值得开采的那个品位？
张博士：完全正确。矿体更准确地应被称为矿化，它包括经济矿石和次经济的低品位材料。矿山开发过程中破碎的低品位材料称为废石或废料。
李先生：那矿石的物理形态呢？我听说过块状矿和浸染矿这两个术语。
张博士：当矿石以相对纯净的形式出现时——例如，几乎是100%的硫化铅（方铅矿）——即使在少量中，也被称为块状矿。
李先生：而浸染矿则相反——矿石稀疏地分布在岩体中？
张博士：正确。如果矿石稀疏地分布在岩体中，使得单个矿石颗粒被高比例的岩石所包围，这就是浸染矿。块状矿通常更容易加工，但如果矿床足够大，浸染矿仍然具有经济价值。
李先生：非常有帮助。现在我对如何分类和评价矿体有了更清晰的认识。

Scene 03
铁帽与表生富集 — Gossan and Supergene Enrichment
工程师们在矿区地表观察铁帽特征，讨论表生富集带与深部原生矿体的关系。
Z Dr. Zhang — 地质工程师
L Mr. Li — 采矿工程师
Z
Dr. Zhang
Look at this outcrop露头 here, Mr. Li. Notice the yellowish-brown, reddish capping覆盖层 on top? This is what we call a gossan铁帽.
L
Mr. Li
It is quite distinctive. How does a gossan form?
Z
Dr. Zhang
Where orebodies outcrop to the surface出露地表, particularly in geologically mature areas free of glacial deposition冰川沉积, the portion near the surface may be leached淋滤 by impure percolating waters渗流水 and subsequently oxidized氧化.
L
Mr. Li
So the valuable minerals are dissolved and carried away, leaving the iron behind?
Z
Dr. Zhang
That is right. Many metallic ores contain a proportion of iron sulphides硫化铁, and these do not leach away but change to iron oxides铁氧化物 — haematite赤铁矿, goethite针铁矿 and jarosite黄钾铁矾. The colour varies slightly with the original mineral content, but the general result is a yellowish, brownish, or reddish ferruginous capping铁质覆盖层 known as a gossan.
L
Mr. Li
So a gossan is essentially an indicator of potential mineralisation beneath the surface. But what happens to the minerals that were leached away?
Z
Dr. Zhang
Excellent question. The minerals leached away from the outcrop often cause a zone of secondary enrichment次生富集, generally in the form of sulphides, at a depth of 60 to 120 metres from the surface. This is known as supergene enrichment表生富集.
L
Mr. Li
So the enrichment zone lies between the leached gossan on top and the unaltered primary orebody原生矿体 below?
Z
Dr. Zhang
Exactly. This enrichment zone then lies on top of the unaltered primary orebody. The supergene zone is often richer than the primary ore, which makes it a priority target for mining. It is important to understand this zonation when planning exploration drilling.
L
Mr. Li
That is fascinating. So finding a gossan at the surface is essentially a clue that there may be a richer orebody at depth. This knowledge will be very useful for our exploration programme.
Z
Dr. Zhang
Precisely. Gossans have historically been one of the most important prospecting找矿 indicators. In fact, many major deposits around the world were first discovered through the observation of iron-capped outcrops.

张博士：李先生，看看这里的露头。注意到顶部那个黄褐色、微红色的覆盖层了吗？这就是我们所说的铁帽。
李先生：确实很特别。铁帽是怎么形成的？
张博士：当矿体出露地表时，特别是在没有冰川沉积的地质成熟地区，靠近地表的部分可能被不纯的渗流水淋滤，随后被氧化。
李先生：所以有价值的矿物被溶解并带走，留下了铁？
张博士：对的。许多金属矿石含有一定比例的硫化铁，这些不会淋滤掉，而是变成铁氧化物——赤铁矿、针铁矿和黄钾铁矾。颜色随原始矿物成分略有变化，但总体结果是形成黄褐色、棕色或红色的铁质覆盖层，即铁帽。
李先生：所以铁帽本质上是地表以下潜在矿化的指示标志。那被淋滤走的矿物去了哪里？
张博士：问得好。从露头淋滤走的矿物通常在距地表60到120米深处造成一个次生富集带，一般以硫化物的形式存在。这就是表生富集。
李先生：所以富集带位于顶部淋滤铁帽和底部未变化的原生矿体之间？
张博士：完全正确。这个富集带位于未变化的原生矿体之上。表生带通常比原生矿石更富，因此是采矿的优先目标。在规划勘探钻孔时理解这种分带很重要。
李先生：太有意思了。所以在地表发现铁帽基本上就是深部可能有更富矿体的线索。这对我们的勘探计划非常有用。
张博士：完全正确。铁帽历来是最重要的找矿标志之一。事实上，世界上许多大型矿床最初就是通过观察铁帽露头发现的。

Scene 04
砂矿勘探 — Placer Exploration
工程师们在河谷地带进行砂矿勘探，讨论冲积矿床和砂矿的成因及分布特征。
Z Dr. Zhang — 地质工程师
L Mr. Li — 采矿工程师
Z
Dr. Zhang
We are now entering an area where placer deposits砂矿 and alluvial deposits冲积矿床 are likely to be found. This river valley is a promising location.
L
Mr. Li
How do placer deposits form, Dr. Zhang? Are they related to the primary deposits we discussed earlier?
Z
Dr. Zhang
Yes, they are directly related. When the primary deposits原生矿床 are brought to the surface by geological methods and then mechanically eroded机械侵蚀, they can suffer one of two fates.
L
Mr. Li
What are the two possible fates?
Z
Dr. Zhang
They may go into solution for subsequent chemical redeposition化学再沉积, or they may break up into fairly stable particles稳定颗粒 which are transported varying distances.
L
Mr. Li
And the placer deposits are formed by the second process — the mechanical breakdown and transport of particles?
Z
Dr. Zhang
Exactly. Heavy mineral particles重矿物颗粒, such as gold, settle close to their source. Lighter particles are carried further downstream. This is why placer gold is often found in river beds and alluvial plains冲积平原 near the original source.
L
Mr. Li
So the distribution of placer minerals depends on the specific gravity of the particles?
Z
Dr. Zhang
That is one important factor. The energy of the transporting medium — water in this case — also plays a role. In areas of high water energy, even heavy particles can be moved, but they tend to settle in traps陷阱（地质） such as behind boulders, in bedrock crevices裂隙, or at bends in the river.
L
Mr. Li
How do placer deposits compare to bedded deposits层状矿床 in terms of formation?
Z
Dr. Zhang
They are quite different. Chemical and physical deposition of consolidated minerals can produce bedded deposits which may extend over larger areas. These are minerals such as coal, iron ore, salt or potash deposits钾盐矿床, and phosphates磷酸盐. Placer deposits, on the other hand, are the result of mechanical erosion and gravity separation.
L
Mr. Li
I see. So for our exploration here, we should focus on sampling the river bed sediments, especially behind those large boulders and in the crevices of the bedrock.
Z
Dr. Zhang
Precisely. Those are the natural trap sites where heavy minerals would accumulate. We should also consider that outcrops露头 occur when a series of beds are cut away by general erosion of the surface or dissected by rivers, so that the edges of beds are exposed. This could also indicate nearby primary mineralisation.

张博士：我们现在进入了一个可能找到砂矿和冲积矿床的区域。这个河谷是一个很有希望的位置。
李先生：砂矿是怎么形成的，张博士？它们和我们之前讨论的原生矿床有关系吗？
张博士：是的，它们直接相关。当原生矿床通过地质作用被带到地表，然后被机械侵蚀后，它们可能面临两种命运。
李先生：哪两种命运？
张博士：它们可能进入溶液中进行随后的化学再沉积，或者可能分解成相当稳定的颗粒，被搬运不同的距离。
李先生：砂矿是由第二种过程形成的——颗粒的机械分解和搬运？
张博士：完全正确。重矿物颗粒，如金，沉降在靠近其来源的地方。较轻的颗粒被搬运到更远的下游。这就是为什么砂金通常在靠近原始来源的河床和冲积平原中被发现。
李先生：所以砂矿的分布取决于颗粒的比重？
张博士：这是一个重要因素。搬运介质的能量——在这里是水——也起着作用。在高水能区域，即使是重颗粒也能被移动，但它们倾向于沉降在陷阱中，如巨石后面、基岩裂隙中或河流弯曲处。
李先生：砂矿和层状矿床在形成方面有什么区别？
张博士：它们有很大不同。固结矿物的化学和物理沉积可以产生层状矿床，可能延伸到大面积区域。这些矿物包括煤、铁矿石、钾盐矿床和磷酸盐。而砂矿则是机械侵蚀和重力分离的结果。
李先生：我明白了。所以在这里的勘探中，我们应该集中采样河床沉积物，特别是那些大巨石后面和基岩裂隙中。
张博士：完全正确。那些是重矿物会聚集的天然陷阱位置。我们还应考虑到，当一系列岩层被地表一般侵蚀切割或被河流切割时，就会出现露头，使岩层边缘暴露出来。这也可能表明附近有原生矿化。
